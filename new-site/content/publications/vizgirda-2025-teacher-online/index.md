abstract: School teachers around the world use online resources in their teaching.
  The education systems in which teachers work directly impact their freedom to choose,
  adapt, and use resources in their classes. This, in turn, influences teachers' information
  interactions involved in lesson planning and resource preparation, but few studies
  have investigated this relationship. Based on a semi-structured interview and observation
  study with 15 school teachers working with the Scottish Curriculum for Excellence,
  a process model is proposed to describe teacher tasks and strategies for finding,
  using, and sharing educational resources. This study addresses the influence of
  the education system context and considers implications for design of search systems
  that support online educational resource search by school teachers. A supplementary
  open licensed dataset can be found at https://osf.io/a2xs8/.
